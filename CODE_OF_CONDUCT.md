# Code of Conduct

meter adopts the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/),
version 2.1, as its code of conduct. The canonical text is linked above; this page summarizes what it
means for this project and how to report a problem.

## The short version

Everyone who takes part in meter — contributors, maintainers, and anyone opening issues or
discussions — is expected to help keep it a respectful, professional, and welcoming space, regardless of
background or identity. Engage in good faith, give and accept constructive feedback gracefully, and keep
the focus on what is best for the project and the people in it.

Conduct that harasses, demeans, or excludes others is not acceptable here, in project spaces or in
public spaces when representing the project. Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject contributions,
comments, and other interactions that violate this code, and may temporarily or permanently restrict
anyone whose behavior they judge harmful.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it privately to the maintainers through the
channel in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) — a GitHub Security Advisory on the repository, or direct contact
with the maintainer. Reports are handled confidentially and reviewed promptly, and the reporter's
privacy and safety are respected.

## Enforcement

Maintainers follow the Contributor Covenant's enforcement guidelines: a graduated response that runs
from a private warning, to a temporary restriction, to a permanent ban, chosen in proportion to the
severity and pattern of the violation. Maintainers are themselves accountable to these standards.
