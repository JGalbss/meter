# Glossary

Every term meter uses, defined once. For how they fit together, read [Concepts](/concepts).

## Ledger and accounts

**ledger** — The immutable double-entry record of every credit movement. The single source of truth;
balances are folded from it, never stored and mutated.

**entry** — One immutable posting against an account, carrying an `EntryType` (`grant`, `usage`,
`reservation_hold`, `settle`, `partial_return`, `void`, `transfer`, `refund`, …) and a signed credit
delta. Entries are never edited or deleted.

**account** — A node in the org → team → user hierarchy that holds credits. Carries a `scope`
(org/team/user/product/session/promo/paid) and an optional `no_overdraft` flag.

**balance** — Derived from an account's entries, not stored. Three views matter:

- **settled** — what has actually been debited or credited.
- **held** — the sum of outstanding reservations.
- **available** — `settled` minus `held`; the figure enforcement reads.

## Credit movements

**grant** — Credits added to an account (a top-up or plan allowance), from a `source` of paid, promo,
or grant.

**reservation** (hold) — A claim on `available` balance for in-flight work, placed before the work runs.
Resolved by a settle or a void.

**settle** — Resolve a reservation to its actual cost, which may be more or less than the hold.

**void** — Release a reservation with no charge.

**void_run** — Reverse the full ledger effect of a run: release its holds and refund its settled
charges, and mark its events voided. The kill switch for a failed run.

**charge** — A direct debit, such as metered usage recorded through `POST /v1/usage`.

**transfer** — A conserving movement between two real accounts. Used by leasing; credits are never
created or destroyed by a transfer.

**lease** — A child (session-scoped) account funded by a `transfer` from a parent, so a long-running
session spends locally without a ledger round-trip per call. Closing it transfers the remainder back.
See [Concepts](/concepts).

## Money and pricing

**credit** — The unit of account inside the engine. Charges, holds, and balances are all in credits.

**credit value** — The USD value of one credit (`METER_CREDIT_VALUE`, default `0.000001`). The divisor
that turns a customer price into credits.

**COGS** — Cost of goods sold: what the model vendor charges you, in USD, summed across priced
dimensions. The basis for margin.

**margin** — The multiplier applied to COGS to get the customer price. A 30% margin is a ×1.30
multiplier.

**rate card** — A versioned, dimensional, priced mapping from usage quantities to a price. `kind` is
`ProviderCost` (the COGS card) or `Customer` (what you bill). See [Pricing](/pricing).

**price component** — One priced dimension of a rate card: a unit price, a charge model, a unit, a
modality, and a context tier.

**dimension** — A countable axis of usage: `InputUncached`, `CacheRead`, `CacheWrite`, `Output`,
`ReasoningOutput`, `ToolCall`, `WebSearch`.

## Events and idempotency

**event** — One immutable usage fact (tokens, an action, an outcome) with custom properties. The system
of record is ClickHouse.

**amend** — Correct an event by recording a *new version* with corrected properties; reads return the
latest non-voided version. The original is never mutated.

**idempotency key** — A caller-supplied key on every mutating call. A replay returns the original result
instead of double-applying — ingest is effectively-once.

**run_id** — Groups the events of one agent run so they can be governed and voided together (`void_run`).

## Policy and billing

**meter** (the noun) — The named label on an event identifying what is being measured (e.g. `tokens`, or
a custom meter). It segments usage for analytics and billing; `POST /v1/usage` records its events under
the `tokens` meter.

**budget** — A time-boxed limit evaluated against an account's usage, returning a status of ok, warning,
or exceeded. Budgets inform; the ledger's `no_overdraft` flag enforces.

**no_overdraft** — An account flag guaranteeing the account can never go negative, even under concurrent
reserves. Property-tested against every ledger backend.

**burnable** — A flag on a usage call. `burnable: false` prices and records the event but does not debit
the ledger (credits charged are 0, the price is still reported).

**invoice** — A deterministic statement summed straight from the ledger over a time window. Because
every charge is an immutable entry, `enforced == billed` by construction.
